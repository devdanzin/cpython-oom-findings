OOM-0033 — import under OOM over-decrefs a sys.path entry; two detection faces
==============================================================================

Setup (both faces): a list slice-assignment makes sys.path a list of mortal 1-char
strings -- `sys.path[:] = "<unicode>"` -- then `__import__(name)` walks that sys.path.
Under the set_nomemory sweep the import's path-based finder over-decrefs a sys.path
entry; the freed/over-decreffed entry is then detected in one of two places.


FACE 1 (segv, the fleet vehicle, ft_release/jit/upstream + sometimes ft_debug_asan):
the corrupted entry is iterated by PathFinder._get_spec and `isinstance(entry, str)`
reads its garbage ob_type -> PyType_IsSubtype derefs a->tp_mro on a bad pointer.

FACE 2 (abort / _Py_NegativeRefcount, deterministic on ft_debug_asan, repro.py 6/6):
the freed entry is decreffed again as a "recycled" old element by the next list
slice-assignment -> negative refcount detected at the recycle-cleanup loop.

Note: a list of *small ints* does NOT expose this -- small ints are immortal, so an
over-decref is a no-op. Mortal entries (sys.path's path strings / the 1-char strings)
are required to surface it.

Matrix (repro.py): ft_debug_asan=abort(negrefcount), jit=segv, upstream=segv, ft_release=no-crash.
